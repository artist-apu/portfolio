About Me

I am a Bangladeshi visual artist whose work explores sustainability, memory, and architectural storytelling. With over 13 years of experience, I focus on transforming waste, particularly plastic and textile remnants, into large-scale artworks that address environmental degradation and social inequity.

Growing up in a country where art is often considered a luxury, I began using inexpensive, accessible materials so that art could belong to everyone. A pivotal moment occurred during a visit to a friend's home in Dhaka, where I witnessed overwhelming plastic pollution. The lives, stories, and surroundings of waste workers inspired me to collect discarded materials and create works that honor their resilience and highlight ecological crisis.

My first large-scale recycled work (25x13 feet) was displayed at the Smart Children’s Carnival, introducing youth to the dangers of plastic waste. I later collaborated with American artist Bhasha Chakrabarti on a textile installation that inspired my own upcycled fabric piece Moharongo.
